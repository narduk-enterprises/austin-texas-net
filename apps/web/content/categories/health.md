## Health in Austin

Austin is one of the fastest-growing cities in America, and with that growth comes an expanding health landscape — from world-class hospitals to persistent seasonal challenges like **cedar fever** and poor air quality days. Whether you're managing allergies, checking current conditions, or looking for care, our health tools keep you informed.

## Austin's Allergy Problem

Austin consistently ranks among the **worst cities in America for allergies**. The combination of geography, climate, and vegetation creates a near-continuous pollen assault from December through May, with cedar and oak as the primary culprits. Hundreds of thousands of Central Texans suffer from seasonal allergies, and newcomers often develop symptoms within their first 1-3 years of exposure.

## Cedar Fever Season

**Mountain cedar** (Ashe Juniper) is Austin's most notorious allergen. Despite its name, cedar fever rarely causes actual fever — but it can produce intense symptoms: sneezing, nasal congestion, itchy eyes, sore throat, and profound fatigue. Cedar season runs from **mid-December through mid-February**, with peak counts in January when cold fronts trigger massive pollen releases.

Pollen counts during peak cedar season routinely exceed **5,000 grains/m³** in Austin, with some days topping 8,000-10,000. For context, most allergy sufferers begin noticing symptoms at just 50 grains/m³.

## Air Quality & Beyond

Austin's air quality is generally good but can spike during **wildfire events**, ozone season (summer), and stagnant weather patterns. Our health dashboard tracks real-time AQI alongside pollen counts so you can make informed decisions about outdoor activity.
